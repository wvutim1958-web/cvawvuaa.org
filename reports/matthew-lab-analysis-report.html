8. üö® Emergency Warning Signs

GO TO THE EMERGENCY ROOM IMMEDIATELY IF YOU EXPERIENCE:

Signs of Acute Pancreatitis (High triglycerides can cause this):

  • Severe abdominal pain (upper abdomen, may radiate to back)
  • Nausea and vomiting (especially if unable to keep food/water down)
  • Fever
  • Rapid pulse
  • Abdominal tenderness

Signs of Heart Attack:

  • Chest pain or pressure (may radiate to arm, jaw, or back)
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cold sweat, nausea, lightheadedness
  • Unusual fatigue (especially in women)

Signs of Stroke:

  • Sudden numbness or weakness (face, arm, or leg, especially one side)
  • Sudden confusion, trouble speaking or understanding
  • Sudden trouble seeing (one or both eyes)
  • Sudden trouble walking, dizziness, loss of balance
  • Sudden severe headache with no known cause
