1. Introduction and Governing Principles

1-1. Purpose and Scope

This handbook outlines the policies, procedures, and expectations for all employees of the City of Bridgeport. These policies apply to all employees unless specifically noted otherwise.

Welcome to the City of Bridgeport

As an employee of the City of Bridgeport, you are part of a dedicated team committed to serving our community. This handbook is designed to help you understand your role, rights, and responsibilities within our organization.

Handbook Application

  • These policies apply to all full-time, part-time, temporary, and seasonal employees
  • Department heads may establish additional procedures specific to their operations
  • Elected officials and certain appointed positions may be exempt from specific provisions
  • Collective bargaining agreements may supersede certain provisions for union employees

Policy Updates

The City maintains the right to modify these policies as needed. Employees will be notified of changes through:

  • Department meetings
  • Email notifications
  • Internal memoranda
  • Updated digital handbook access

1-2. Employment Relationship

At-Will Employment

Employment with the City of Bridgeport is on an "at-will" basis unless otherwise specified in a written employment agreement. This means that either the employee or the City may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause or advance notice.

Employment Agreement Exceptions

  • Union employees covered by collective bargaining agreements
  • Employees with specific term contracts
  • Positions governed by City Charter provisions
  • Special project positions with defined terms

Changes to Employment Terms

The City reserves the right to modify the terms of employment including:

  • Job duties and responsibilities
  • Work location and schedule
  • Compensation and benefits
  • Reporting relationships

1-3. Equal Employment Opportunity

Non-Discrimination Policy

The City of Bridgeport is committed to providing equal employment opportunities to all individuals regardless of:

  • Race, color, or national origin
  • Religion or creed
  • Sex, gender identity, or sexual orientation
  • Age (40 or older)
  • Disability (physical or mental)
  • Genetic information
  • Veteran or military status
  • Any other protected characteristic under federal, state, or local law

Areas of Equal Opportunity

This policy extends to all terms and conditions of employment, including:

  • Recruitment and hiring
  • Training and development
  • Promotion and transfer
  • Compensation and benefits
  • Work environment and conditions
  • Disciplinary actions
  • Termination of employment

Reasonable Accommodations

The City will provide reasonable accommodations to:

  • Qualified individuals with disabilities
  • Employees with religious requirements
  • Pregnancy and related conditions

Reporting Discrimination

Employees who believe they have experienced discrimination should:

  • Report the incident to their immediate supervisor
  • Contact the Human Resources Department
  • File a formal complaint if necessary
  • Cooperate with any investigation

Non-Retaliation

The City strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee who:

  • Reports discrimination or harassment
  • Files a complaint
  • Participates in an investigation
  • Opposes discriminatory practices

2. Employment Policies

2-1. Hiring and Onboarding

Recruitment Process

The City follows a structured recruitment process to ensure fair and effective hiring:

  • Job posting requirements:
    • Internal posting for 5 business days
    • External advertising for minimum 10 business days
    • Posting on City website and approved job boards
    • Clear description of qualifications and responsibilities
  • Application review:
    • Initial screening by HR Department
    • Qualification verification
    • Selection of interview candidates
    • Communication with applicants
  • Interview process:
    • Structured interview panels
    • Skills assessment when applicable
    • Reference checks
    • Final selection approval

Pre-Employment Requirements

All employment offers are contingent upon successful completion of:

  • Background screening:
    • Criminal history check
    • Employment verification
    • Education verification
    • Professional license validation
  • Drug screening:
    • DOT requirements for applicable positions
    • Safety-sensitive position screening
    • Random testing program participation
  • Physical examination:

    • Functional capacity testing for specific roles

Onboarding Process

New employees must complete the following within their first week:

    • Job-specific skills training
    • Safety certification if required

2-2. Employment Classifications

Employment Status Categories

Employees are classified according to the following categories:

Regular Full-Time
  • Works 40 hours per week
  • Eligible for all benefits
  • Paid time off accrual
  • Holiday pay eligibility
Regular Part-Time
  • Less than 40 hours per week
  • Pro-rated benefits if 20+ hours
  • Scheduled hours requirements
  • Holiday pay eligibility if qualified
Temporary
  • Limited duration employment
  • Project-specific assignments
  • Seasonal positions
  • No benefits eligibility

FLSA Classifications

Positions are classified as either:

Exempt
  • Qualifications:
    • Salary basis payment
    • Minimum salary threshold
    • Duties test requirements
    • Professional, Executive, or Administrative roles
  • Provisions:
    • No overtime pay
    • Full-day salary deductions only
    • Professional time management
Non-Exempt
  • Requirements:
    • Overtime eligible
    • Time tracking mandatory
    • Meal/break period compliance
  • Compensation:
    • Overtime at 1.5x regular rate
    • Paid for all hours worked
    • Call-back pay provisions

5. Leave Policies

5-1. Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)

FMLA Eligibility

  • Basic requirements:
    • 12 months of employment
    • 1,250 hours worked in past 12 months
    • 50 employees within 75 miles
  • Qualifying conditions:
    • Birth or adoption of a child
    • Serious health condition (self)
    • Family member's serious health condition
    • Military qualifying exigency
    • Military caregiver leave (26 weeks)

Leave Administration

  • Request procedures:
    • 30-day advance notice when foreseeable
    • Medical certification requirements
    • Fitness for duty certification
    • Periodic status updates
  • Leave duration:
    • 12 weeks in 12-month period
    • Intermittent leave options
    • Reduced schedule availability

Benefits During Leave

  • Health insurance:
    • Continuation of coverage
    • Premium payment arrangements
    • Recovery of premiums if no return
  • Other benefits:
    • Vacation/sick leave accrual
    • Holiday pay eligibility
    • Service time calculations

Return to Work

    • Medical clearance if applicable

9. Closing Words and Glossary

This handbook represents the current policies and procedures of the City of Bridgeport. The City reserves the right to modify, change, or update these policies at any time to meet organizational needs. Employees will be notified of any significant changes.

Key Terms

  • At-Will Employment: Employment relationship that can be terminated by either party
  • Exempt/Non-Exempt: FLSA classifications for overtime eligibility
  • FMLA: Family and Medical Leave Act protections
  • Progressive Discipline: Stepped approach to performance improvement
  • Protected Class: Characteristics protected from discrimination
  • Reasonable Accommodation: Workplace changes to support disability or religious needs
