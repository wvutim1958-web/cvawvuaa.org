Basic FMLA Definitions

Original Text:

To care for the employee's spouse, son, daughter, or parent (not in-law) who has a serious health condition.

Proposed Update:

To care for the employee's spouse, son, daughter, or parent who has a serious health condition. For FMLA purposes:

  • 'Spouse' means a husband or wife as defined or recognized in the state where the individual was married, including same-sex marriage or common law marriage where recognized
  • 'Son or daughter' means a biological, adopted, or foster child, a stepchild, a legal ward, or a child of a person standing in loco parentis, regardless of age or dependency status
  • 'Parent' means a biological, adoptive, step or foster father or mother, or any other individual who stood in loco parentis to the employee (does not include parents 'in-law')

Rationale:

Updates align with current federal law and Supreme Court decisions regarding same-sex marriage. Clearer definitions help prevent misunderstandings and potential discrimination claims. Added specific inclusions protect both employees and the City by clearly defining eligible relationships.

Military Family Leave

Original Text:

A "covered servicemember" is a current member of the Armed Forces, including a member of the National Guard or Reserves, who is undergoing medical treatment, recuperation, or therapy...

Proposed Update:

A "covered servicemember" means:

  1. A current member of the Armed Forces, including a member of the National Guard or Reserves, who is undergoing medical treatment, recuperation, or therapy, is in outpatient status, or is on the temporary disability retired list, for a serious injury or illness; or
  2. A veteran discharged or released under conditions other than dishonorable within the five-year period before the first date the eligible employee takes FMLA leave, who is undergoing medical treatment, recuperation, or therapy for a serious injury or illness.

Qualifying exigencies may include:

  • Short-notice deployment (up to 7 days)
  • Military events and related activities
  • Childcare and school activities
  • Financial and legal arrangements
  • Counseling
  • Rest and recuperation (up to 15 days)
  • Post-deployment activities
  • Parental care
  • Additional activities agreed upon by the City and employee

Rationale:

Expands and clarifies military family leave provisions to fully comply with current FMLA regulations. Added specific qualifying exigencies provides clear guidance for both employees and managers. Including veterans ensures proper coverage for those transitioning from military service.

4. Wage and Hour Provisions

Overtime and Compensatory Time

Original Text:

Non-exempt employees will be paid overtime at a rate of one and one-half times their regular rate for hours worked in excess of 40 in a workweek. Overtime must be approved in advance by the supervisor.

Proposed Update:

Overtime Compensation
  • Non-exempt employees receive:
    • 1.5 times regular rate for hours over 40 in a workweek
    • 2.0 times regular rate for hours over 12 in a workday
    • 1.5 times regular rate for all hours worked on holidays
  • Overtime Calculation:
    • Includes non-discretionary bonuses
    • Includes shift differentials
    • Includes on-call pay
    • Regular rate recalculation for variable compensation
Compensatory Time
  • Eligible employees may elect compensatory time in lieu of overtime pay
  • Accrual limit: 240 hours (480 for public safety)
  • Must be used within 12 months of accrual
  • Cash payment for unused comp time upon separation
Authorization and Recording
  • Prior authorization required except in emergencies
  • Unauthorized overtime must still be paid
  • Accurate recording of all hours worked required
  • Disciplinary action for working unauthorized overtime

Rationale:

Enhanced overtime provisions provide clear guidance while ensuring FLSA compliance. Added specific provisions for various types of premium pay and compensatory time options. Clear authorization requirements protect both employees and the City.

Meal and Rest Breaks

Original Text:

Employees are entitled to a 30-minute unpaid meal break for shifts over 6 hours. Additional breaks may be provided as determined by the supervisor.

Proposed Update:

Required Breaks
  • Meal Breaks:
    • 30-minute unpaid break for shifts over 6 hours
    • Must be taken between 3rd and 5th hour of shift
    • Second meal break for shifts over 10 hours
    • Must be uninterrupted and duty-free
  • Rest Breaks:
    • 15-minute paid break per 4 hours worked
    • Must be taken in middle of work period
    • Cannot be combined with meal breaks
    • Cannot be used to leave early
Break Time Compensation
  • Meal breaks are unpaid if:
    • At least 30 minutes long
    • Employee is completely relieved of duties
    • Employee is free to leave work area
  • Break time is paid if:
    • Employee performs any work during break
    • Break is less than 30 minutes
    • Employee must remain on premises

Rationale:

Updated to ensure compliance with labor laws while providing clear guidance on break requirements. Added specific criteria for paid vs. unpaid time to prevent wage-hour violations. Clear structure helps supervisors manage breaks effectively.

Timekeeping and Records

Original Text:

Employees must accurately record all time worked. Falsification of time records will result in disciplinary action.

Proposed Update:

Time Recording Requirements
  • All hours worked must be recorded, including:
    • Pre-shift and post-shift work
